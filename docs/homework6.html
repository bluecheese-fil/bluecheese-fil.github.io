Gambler's Ruin Problem


Gambler's Ruin Problem is a classic problem in probability theory. In this famous scenario, a gambler starts with an initial amount of i dollars. On each game, the gambler wins 1$ with probability p and loses 1$ with probability
q = p - 1
. We have to keep in mind that both p and q are probability numbers, therefore must be defined as bigger or equals than 0 and smaller or equals than 1. The gambler will stop playing once he has lost all his money, or has accumulated N dollars with his wins. This problem's question aims to find the probability that the gambler will end up with N dollars.
