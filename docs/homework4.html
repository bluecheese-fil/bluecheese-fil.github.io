Law of Large Numbers (LLN)
The Law of Large Numbers states that, as the number of indipendent and identically distributed random variables increases, the average will converge to the expected value of the underlying distribution. This means that, by repeating an exeperiment a large amount of times, the average of the results is equals to the expected value. This means that
\lim_{n\to+\infty}\sum{\frac{X_{i}}{n}} = \overline{X}

In particular, we can consider the weak and strong law of large numbers:
The weak law states that the sample average converse in probability towards the expected value. This means that, for any positive number Îµ,
\lim_{n\to+\infty}Pr(|\overline{X_{n}} - \mu < \varepsilon|) = 1
The strong law states that the sample average converges almost surely to the expected value. That is
Pr(\lim_{n\to+\infty}\overline{X_{n}} = \mu) = 1

Central Limit Theorem (CLT)
The central limit theorem states that, if you take sufficiently large samples from a population, the samples mean will be normally distributed, even if the population isn't normally distributed (1). This relates to our exercise considering that, increasing the number of attackers and systems, the distribution resembled a normal distribution.
