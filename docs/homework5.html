Poisson Point Process


In statistics a Poisson Point Process is a kind of random mathematical object. It consists of points randomly located on a mathematical space, where each point is indipendent from each other. This is often called Poisson random measure or, simply, Poisson process. This process is characterized via the Poisson distribution, where the probability is dipended on N (called a Poisson random variable). Therefore, the probability of non-overlapping regions is:
Pr\left\{ N = n \right\}=\frac{\lambda^{n}}{n!}e^{-\lambda}
This is the probability that the Nth number is equal to n. The other variables are λ , the parameter of the Poisson distribution, e the Euler's constant and n , which is the number of events that we are calculating. To recap, the properties that a Poisson Process follows are:
  • Indipendece.
  • Homogeneity , since the points are distributed uniformally throughout the space or time interval.
  • Poisson property
